Artist Bio

I’m Jackson Domenico, a 21-year-old senior at the University of Florida (UF) pursuing a BFA in ceramics. I also work with fiber art, painting, drawing, sculpture, and installation. My work explores themes like queerness, community, craft, neurodivergence, memory, and environmental ecology. I’m especially interested in the connection between queer people and nature, which shows up in botanical motifs and references to biological processes on micro and macro scales.

Artist Statement

My work centers on themes of community, craft, neurodivergence, queerness, and environmental ecology, expressed through painting, sculpture, ceramics, installation, and fiber art. Vibrant neon colors, clashing patterns, and soft, rounded forms create a sense of play, comfort, and connection. These abstract vessels explore ideas of community and personhood, inviting a tender reception from viewers. Balancing fine art and craft, I see this "in-between" space as a metaphor for queerness and authenticity. Craft ties me to a community, creating a shared lineage of specialized labor passed down through generations. My process embraces imperfections and minimizes waste by repurposing byproducts, echoing the natural cycles of ecosystems. Each scrap of material carries meaning, reflecting my commitment to leaving nothing behind.
